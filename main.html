KIRKLAND COMMUNITY

General information on Kirkland

Kirkland, Washington, is a scenic city on Lake Washington, east of Seattle, known for its vibrant downtown waterfront, strong economy driven by tech and retail, and high quality of life, with a population over 92,000 as of the 2020 census. Founded by Peter Kirk in 1888 for a steel mill, it's now a major Seattle suburb offering extensive parks, recreational trails like the Cross Kirkland Corridor, and proximity to major tech companies, attracting professionals and residents seeking a blend of natural beauty and urban amenities.

Key characteristics

ocation: Northeast shore of Lake Washington, a suburb of Seattle. Population: Around 92,000 (2020 Census), making it a significant King County city. Economy: Strong tech presence (Google, Tableau), retail, healthcare, and a thriving local business scene. Lifestyle: Walkable downtown, waterfront parks (Marina Park), beaches, public art, and numerous festivals (Oktoberfest, Film Festival). History: Named after Peter Kirk, who established a steel mill; the city grew from this industrial base. Transportation: Accessible via I-405, I-5, and State Route 520, offering convenient access to Seattle and Bellevue

Notable features

Parks & Recreation: Over 50 parks, including waterfront spaces, and the popular Cross Kirkland Corridor. Downtown: Charming, with restaurants, boutiques, and art galleries. Community: A diverse community attracting tech professionals, artists, and families. Brand Association: Home to the popular "Kirkland Signature" brand from Costco, which once had its main office there.
